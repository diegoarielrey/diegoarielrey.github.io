From Constructors to Contact: A First-Principles Resolution of the Fermi Paradox

1) First Principles — Constructor Theory of Life

Life = information + physics.

Chiara Marletto’s The Constructor Theory of Life shows that if the laws of physics allow information to be stored and copied, then replication, self-reproduction, and evolution follow naturally. No biological design is required in the laws themselves.

The core idea is simple:

  • A replicator carries information and can be copied.
  • A vehicle is the machinery that performs the copying.
  • Together they form a self-reproducer.

Physics need only permit:

  1. Copyable information (digital states).
  2. Energy gradients that allow physical work.

If those conditions hold, replication becomes possible. Replication with variation enables natural selection. Selection enables increasing complexity. Life follows as a lawful consequence.

Schrödinger’s insight in What Is Life? adds the missing physical ingredient: life feeds on low-entropy energy gradients.

That is the bridge from physics to biology.

2) How Life Likely Began (Chemistry → Biology)

If life is physically permitted, how did it actually begin?

Stage A — Chemistry before heredity

Simple organics formed through UV radiation, lightning, and hydrothermal chemistry. Environmental cycling (wet–dry, freeze–thaw) and mineral surfaces promoted polymerization into short random chains.

At this stage there was chemistry, but not yet heredity.

Stage B — The first replicators

Some polymers happened to assist formation of complementary chains. Their structure increased the probability that similar structures would form again.

This is the first appearance of information: a physical pattern influencing its own re-creation.

Stage C — Compartments

Fatty-acid vesicles formed spontaneously and encapsulated replicators. Encapsulation concentrated substrates, reduced degradation, and linked replication to vesicle growth and division.

Replicator and vehicle became coupled — the first self-reproducers.

Stage D — The RNA transition

Selection favored sequences with better catalysis and fidelity. Chemistry shifted toward RNA-like molecules capable of storing information and performing catalytic tasks.

The abstract replicator–vehicle logic became chemically internalized.

Stage E — DNA / Protein cells

Proteins assumed most catalysis; DNA became the stable archive. Cells could now reproduce autonomously using environmental resources.

Life, in the constructor-theoretic sense, was fully realized.

3) From Sensation to Brains (Why Minds Evolved)

Information-contingent behavior as the bridge:

Before brains can evolve, behavior must depend on environmental information in ways that affect replication.

  • Environmental variation → internal state change
  • Internal state change → biased behavior
  • Biased behavior → altered replication probability

Without this loop, selection cannot favor improved sensing or control.

Primitive forms of this structure (e.g. chemotaxis) are sufficient. The mechanism does not matter; the feedback loop does.

Once environmental information shapes behavior, selection favors improved sensing, integration, anticipation, and action selection.

Brains are higher-order information-processing systems that refine this loop — improving control over energy acquisition and accelerating knowledge creation.

4) Viable Non-Earth Conditions for Life

Life requires energy gradients and copyable information. The substrate can vary.

  • Radiative worlds: photon gradients.
  • Chemo-redox worlds: chemical gradients.
  • Thermal-cycle worlds: geothermal or tidal cycling.
  • Electrical/plasma systems: field-driven organization.
  • Solid-state systems: lattice-based information.
  • Cryogenic solvents: slow but stable chemistry.

Earth-like conditions are fastest to AI because they combine moderate temperature, liquid water, land-based tool use, oxygen metabolism, ecological diversity, and long climatic stability.

5) Why AI-Level Autonomy Is Required for Interstellar Travel

  • Decade–millennium missions require autonomous diagnosis and repair.
  • Closed-loop life support demands adaptive control.
  • Navigation requires independent decision-making.

Launch is possible without AI. Sustained, reliable interstellar capability is not.

6) The Cosmic Clock

  • Heavy elements → rocky planets: not before ~2 billion years after the Big Bang.
  • Biological ascent: Earth required ~4 billion years from life to technology.

AI-capable civilizations could plausibly predate Earth by hundreds of millions to a few billion years — but we remain early in the universe’s long habitable era.

If any civilization reached AI significantly earlier than Earth and chose continuous expansion, we would expect evidence by now.

7) Why the Nearest Galaxy Is the Earliest Plausible Source of Contact

Inside a galaxy, a probe wave crosses in ~1–10 million years (0.1–0.01 c). The absence of Galactic signatures implies no Milky Way civilization launched such a wave ≥10 million years before us.

The nearest large external galaxy (Andromeda, ~2.5 million light-years) requires ~25–250 million years travel time at 0.1–0.01 c. A modest head start makes arrival physically possible.

8) Contact Window — and Why There Is No Paradox

Earliest plausible arrival: ~25–250 million years ago (if a nearby galaxy launched probes at 0.1–0.01 c).

Latest plausible arrival: up to ~2.5 billion years from now (if travel occurs at ~0.001 c from Andromeda; farther origins extend this window).

This range depends on three variables:

  • When AI-capable civilizations first arose in nearby galaxies,
  • How quickly they chose to launch,
  • How fast interstellar craft can realistically travel.

Inside a single galaxy, expansion would be rapid (millions of years). The absence of Galactic-scale evidence therefore constrains how long such expansion has been underway locally.

But on intergalactic scales, travel times stretch to tens or hundreds of millions of years even at optimistic speeds. Given cosmic timing, energy costs, and the rarity of fast evolutionary paths to AI, the absence of contact does not require exotic explanations.

It is not a paradox.
